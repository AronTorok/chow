CHOWDER MUNG-DAAL SCHNITZEL TRUFFLES

The goal of this website is to showcase the effect known as "unmoving plaid" (this effect has many other names). You can read more about it on the TVTropes website. I chose the Cartoon Network show called "Chowder", because this effect is very prominent in it.

CHOWDER

Pay attention to his hat and his long sleeve shirt.

Chowder is the titular main protagonist of the series of the same name. He is an apprentice under the learning of Mung Daal. In several episodes, there is a gag that Chowder is fully aware he is in a cartoon (commonly called "breaking the fourth wall"). However, this does not seem to bother him, except for the episode "Brain Grub".

MUNG-DAAL

Look at his orange and green plaid-patterned kilt.

Mung Daal is the owner and head chef of Mung Daal's Catering Company, and the deuteragonist of the series. Mung is typically kind and friendly and has exhibited a good amount of patience for his apprentice and his outlandish antics. He can, however, be quite stern if that patience reaches its limit.

SCHNITZEL

Watch his entire skin

Shnitzel (alternatively spelled as “Schnitzel") is the tritagonist of Chowder. He is an employee of Mung Daal Catering who is not only underpaid but has to endure the daily shenanigans of his boss's apprentice, Chowder. Often the straightman of the series, Shnitzel frequently regards Mung's and Chowder's behavior as questionable or bizarre.

TRUFFLES

Observe her hair.

Truffles Daal (neé Fungstein) is the tritagonist of Chowder and Mung Daal's wife. While her husband handles all the cooking for the catering company, Truffles handles more of the financial- and business-related aspects of it (including serving as the cashier at Mung Daal's Catering).
